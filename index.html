Face and Eye Movement Tracking

Face and eye movement tracking is a technology that captures and analyzes the position and movement of facial features, including eyes, using cameras and sensors. It often relies on computer vision and machine learning to interpret these movements in real-time. Applications include emotion recognition, attention analysis, and human-computer interaction. Commonly used in areas like gaming, virtual reality, and assistive technologies, it enhances user experience and accessibility. Advanced systems can even detect subtle changes, such as gaze direction or pupil dilation, for deeper insights.

Rainfall prediction

Rainfall prediction involves estimating future rainfall based on meteorological data and models. It uses inputs like temperature, humidity, wind speed, and historical rainfall patterns. Techniques range from traditional statistical methods to advanced machine learning and AI models. Accurate predictions help in agriculture, water resource management, and disaster preparedness. Challenges include variability in weather patterns and limited data in some regions.

Traffic prediction

Traffic prediction involves estimating future traffic conditions, such as vehicle flow, speed, and congestion levels, using historical and real-time data. It employs machine learning, statistical models, and algorithms to analyze patterns and trends. Accurate predictions help in route planning, reducing congestion, and optimizing traffic management. Advanced techniques like deep learning and IoT sensors improve prediction accuracy by integrating diverse data sources. These insights support smarter cities and enhance transportation efficiency
